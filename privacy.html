Privacy Policy

Effective: May 22, 2026  |  Previous version: May 14, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Matthew Ryan Grimmett, an individual operating in West Virginia, United States ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you use the ShiftSnap mobile application (the "App"). We respect your privacy and only collect the minimum data needed for the App to function.

Short version: We collect only what you give us. We don't sell your data. We don't run ads. You can delete everything at any time.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

When you sign up, we collect:

  • Email address
  • Full name (if you choose to provide it, or via Sign in with Apple)
  • An encrypted password (handled by Supabase Auth — we never see plaintext passwords)

1.2 Work & Shift Data

The App stores everything you create inside it:

  • Jobs (name, employer, hourly rate, color, icon)
  • Shifts (clock-in and clock-out times, hours worked, breaks)
  • Notes (text you type during a shift)
  • Photos and videos you attach to shifts
  • Expenses you log
  • Tax settings (federal / state / local / FICA rates you configure)
  • Pay schedule (period type and next pay date)

1.3 Location Data (optional, Pro feature)

If you enable GPS Location Logging in Settings, the App records your device's latitude and longitude when you clock in. Location is never recorded passively in the background — only when you take an action that requests it. You can disable GPS logging at any time in Settings → Premium Tools → GPS Logging.

1.4 Subscription & Purchase Data

Subscription status is managed by Apple In-App Purchase and RevenueCat. We receive the entitlement status ("Pro" vs. "Free"), product identifier, renewal date, and an anonymous user identifier — but we do not see your credit card or full Apple ID. All payment processing is handled by Apple.

1.5 Diagnostic Data

We may receive crash reports and basic diagnostic information from Expo and Apple to fix bugs. This data is aggregated and does not identify you personally.

4. Data Retention

We keep your data for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account, all your data is removed from our active databases within 30 days. Backups are rotated and fully overwritten within 90 days.

5. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you have different rights over your data. We honour all of them regardless of location.

5.1 Rights for all users

  • Access — request a copy of all data we hold about you.
  • Correction — ask us to fix inaccurate information.
  • Deletion — delete your account and all associated data inside the App, or email us.
  • Export — download your data (PDF timesheets and CSV exports are available inside the App).

5.2 Additional rights for EEA, UK & Swiss residents (GDPR / UK GDPR)

  • Right to restriction — ask us to pause processing while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw at any time.
  • Right to lodge a complaint — you have the right to file a complaint with your local data protection authority (e.g. ICO in the UK, or your EU member state's authority).

5.3 Additional rights for California residents (CCPA / CPRA)

  • Right to know — request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected.
  • Right to delete — request deletion of your personal information.
  • Right to correct — request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing — we do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising.
  • Right to non-discrimination — we will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights.

5.4 Additional rights for Canadian residents (PIPEDA)

  • You have the right to access personal information we hold about you and to challenge its accuracy.
  • You may withdraw consent to our collection or use of your personal information at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions, by contacting us.

5.5 Additional rights for Australian residents (Privacy Act 1988)

  • You have the right to access and seek correction of personal information we hold about you in accordance with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).
  • You may complain to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) if you believe we have breached the APPs.

To exercise any of these rights, email shiftsnapsupport@gmail.com. We respond within 30 days (or within 1 month for GDPR requests).

6. International Data Transfers

ShiftSnap is operated from the United States. Our infrastructure providers (Supabase, RevenueCat, Expo) may process your data in the United States or other countries. Where data is transferred from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland to countries without an adequacy decision, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or other appropriate safeguards as required by applicable law. By using the App, you acknowledge that your data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your own.

7. Children's Privacy

ShiftSnap is not intended for children under 13 (or under 16 where required by local law). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children below these age thresholds. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

8. Security

All traffic between the App and our backend uses HTTPS/TLS encryption. Passwords are hashed with industry-standard algorithms by Supabase. Data at rest is encrypted on Supabase's servers. No system is 100% secure — if you suspect your account has been compromised, change your password immediately and email us.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be announced inside the App. The "Effective" date at the top of this page indicates when the latest version took effect. Where required by law, we will obtain your consent before making material changes.
